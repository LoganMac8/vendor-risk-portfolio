## üß† Key Takeaways
- SOC 2 is less about checking boxes and more about evaluating whether an organization can **consistently protect customer data**.  
- Type II is significantly more valuable than Type I in Vendor Risk assessments.  
- The Trust Service Criteria form the backbone of SOC 2; most controls map directly to one or more criteria.  
- Understanding the structure of a SOC 2 report is critical for reading findings, exceptions, and control gaps.  

---

## üîç What I Found Interesting
- The Security category overlaps heavily with ISO 27001 Annex A controls.  
- Many companies use the same evidence (MFA, access reviews, encryption policies) to satisfy both SOC 2 and ISO requirements.  
- The biggest value of SOC 2 is how it forces vendors to **document, monitor, and prove** their security practices.
